# Fable 1

## Original (English)

In a magical greenhouse, where sunlight danced through crystal prisms and colorful flowers bloomed, a humble spider spun her web. She lived among the gentle bees and busy butterflies, who respected her quiet ways.

One day, a greedy robber bee, known for stealing nectar from the flowers, and a sly praying mantis, infamous for snatching unsuspecting insects, threatened the greenhouse. They planned to steal the precious pollen and leave the garden bare.

The spider, usually content to spin her web, saw the danger. She remembered how the bees and butterflies had helped her when a fierce storm destroyed her home. The spider decided to warn her friends and unite them against the threat.

Together, the unlikely allies created a strong defense. The spider's web caught the greedy robber bee, while the busy butterflies chased the sly praying mantis. The garden's creatures worked together, using their unique skills to protect their home.

The robber bee and praying mantis were forced to help their former victims gather pollen and tend to the flowers. As they worked, they realized how their greed had put the garden in danger. They apologized and vowed to repay their debt by helping the garden grow strong.

The spider smiled, seeing the robber bee and praying mantis care for the flowers. "We can all work together," she said. "When we share and help, everyone is happy and healthy." The garden's creatures cheered, grateful for the spider's wisdom. From that day on, the greenhouse flourished, and the robber bee and praying mantis became valuable friends, showing that generosity can quell even the greatest greed.

# Fable 2

## Original (English)

In a magical greenhouse, a spoiled beaver loved to play with the colorful flowers and vines. The beaver would often knock over delicate stems, and break off sparkling crystals that hung from the ceiling. But when the beaver's friends asked to play with the crystals, the beaver would say, "No, they're mine!"

One day, the beaver's friends asked again, "Please, can we see the crystals?" But the beaver said, "No, go away!" The beaver's friends looked sad and left the greenhouse. The beaver felt happy, thinking, "I have all the crystals to myself!"

As the days passed, the beaver noticed that the greenhouse was getting dusty and the plants were starting to wilt. The beaver's friends didn't come to play anymore, and the greenhouse felt empty. The beaver realized that they had been too mean to their friends, and that's why they didn't want to play together.

The beaver decided to share the crystals with their friends. "Let's share them together!" exclaimed the beaver. The friends were overjoyed, and they all played happily in the greenhouse. As they played, the greenhouse began to shine and sparkle again. The beaver's friends helped the beaver clean the greenhouse, and together they made it more beautiful than ever.

The beaver was happy to see their friends smiling and playing together. "I'm glad I shared the crystals with you," said the beaver. "Now the greenhouse is full of joy, and I have even more beautiful things to play with!"

The beaver learned that when you share with others, you get to share in the happiness and beauty that comes with it. The beaver's friends agreed, and from that day on, they all played together, sharing and caring for the magical greenhouse.

# Fable 3

## Original (English)

In the heart of a lush forest, near a winding river, lay a cozy remote hamlet. The hamlet was home to many animals, including a hardworking hippopotamus. The hippopotamus spent her days tending to her garden, growing juicy fruits and vegetables for her friends and family.

One day, while exploring the forest, the hippopotamus stumbled upon a magical seed. The seed promised to grow into a tree that would produce the most delicious and sought-after fruits in the land. The hippopotamus, driven by her love for gardening, decided to plant the seed in her garden.

As the seed began to grow, the hippopotamus became increasingly obsessed with the thought of having the rarest fruits in the land. She imagined the envy and admiration of her friends and the praise of the hamlet's animals. Her greed grew, and she started to neglect her other plants, focusing solely on the magical tree.

However, as the tree grew, so did its demands. It required more and more water, and the hippopotamus had to sacrifice her other plants to satisfy its needs. The tree's fruits were not as delectable as promised, and the hamlet's animals were not impressed.

One rainy day, a strong wind knocked the magical tree to the ground, destroying it. The hippopotamus was devastated. But as she looked around at her once-thriving garden, now overgrown and neglected, she realized the harm her greed had caused. She began to tend to her garden again, and this time, she worked with love and care, not fear and obsession. The hippopotamus learned that fear limits growth, and with humility, her garden flourished once more.

# Fable 4

## Original (English)

On a stormy day, a Humble Ostrich stood at the edge of a rain-soaked cliff. The strong wind whipped her long neck back and forth, but she held tight. Below her, a small cave hid a treasure trove left by a wise old bird.

The Humble Ostrich had heard that her cousin, Proud Peacock, was coming to the cliff to claim the treasure. She worried that they would fight over it. As the wind howled, the Humble Ostrich decided to get there first.

Just as she was about to enter the cave, Proud Peacock appeared, shaking the rain off his feathers. "This treasure is mine!" he shouted. "I need it more than you do!"

The Humble Ostrich stood her ground. "Why do you need it so much?" she asked. Proud Peacock explained that he wanted to buy a new shiny coat to show off to his friends. The Humble Ostrich thought for a moment. "I want the treasure too," she said, "but not for myself. I want to use it to build a safe home for the birds who lost theirs in the storm."

Proud Peacock's feathers ruffled with excitement. "That's a wonderful idea!" he exclaimed. Together, they divided the treasure and built a cozy shelter for their friends. As they worked, the rain slowed to a gentle patter.

The Humble Ostrich and Proud Peacock looked at each other and smiled. "We might have fought over the treasure," the Humble Ostrich said, "but working together gave us something even better." Proud Peacock nodded in agreement. "Sometimes, giving up our own way can lead to a new path, one that's even more wonderful than we imagined."

## Translation (Romanian)

Într-o zi furtunoasă, o Struț umilă stătea la marginea unei stânci îmbibate de ploaie. Vântul puternic îi mișca lungul gât înapoi și-nainte, dar ea se ține țuguiată. Mai jos, o peșteră mică ascundea o comoară lăsată de o pasăre înțeleaptă.

Struța umilă auzise că vărul ei, Mândrul Păun, urma să ajungă la stâncă pentru a revendica comoara. Îi era teamă că se vor certa pentru ea. În timp ce vântul urla, Struța umilă a decis să ajungă acolo mai întâi.

Struța umilă nu s-a lăsat intimidată. „De ce ai așa de mare nevoie de ea?” întrebă ea. Mândrul Păun explică că voia să cumpere o haină nouă, sclipitoare, pentru a-și impresiona prietenii. # Fable 5

## Original (English)

**The Tale of the Sneaky Gecko**

In a colorful underwater reef, where fish of all kinds swam together, a cunning gecko lived. He loved to sneak up on his friends and snatch their shiny shells. The gecko thought it was the most fun game ever!

One sunny day, while the gecko was out playing, he saw a beautiful pearl shell lying on the ocean floor. His eyes sparkled with mischief, and he quickly snatched it. "Ha! All mine!" he squeaked.

But, when he swam away with the shell, his friend, a wise old fish, saw what had happened. "Hey, that's not yours!" the fish said. "You took my friend's shell without asking!"

The gecko looked around, hoping no one had seen him. But, the wise old fish had already told all his friends what happened. They gathered around, demanding that the gecko return the shell.

At first, the gecko denied taking the shell. "I didn't do it! I'm a good gecko!" he said. But, the wise old fish showed him the evidence – a glint of the pearl shell in his claws.

The gecko felt ashamed of his mistake. He apologized to his friends and returned the shell. From that day on, the gecko learned to always ask before taking something that didn't belong to him.

The other sea creatures smiled at the gecko, happy to see him change. They learned that saying please and thank you is always the best way to make friends. And, the gecko discovered that courtesy is never wasted – it helps you keep your friends and makes everyone happy!

Wise Wings' rival, a sly snake named Slithy, saw an opportunity to cause trouble. He whispered, "I know Wise Wings' secret stash. Meet me at the old oak tree if you want to see it!" Many birds followed Slithy to the old oak, but when they got there, they found nothing but an old, hollow log.

Wise Wings arrived, and instead of being angry, she asked Slithy, "Why did you lie?" Slithy said, "I wanted to see what would happen." Wise Wings replied, "We made fools of ourselves, didn't we?" Slithy nodded, and from that day on, he and Wise Wings became unlikely friends.

The birds learned that they should think carefully before believing rumors. Wise Wings showed that a bright mind can see beyond illusions, and that friends can become allies when they work together.

One day, two families moved into the warehouse. The first family had two little rabbits, who were very kind to the hare. They shared their carrots and played with her every day. The second family had two strong, gruff squirrels who liked to play rough games and chase the hare.

The hare was torn between her two new families. She loved playing with the rabbits, but she also enjoyed the squirrels' exciting games. But then, a big storm came, and the warehouse began to leak. The rabbits' home was flooded, and they needed help.

The squirrels, who were the best climbers, offered to help the rabbits fix their home. The hare was worried about siding with one family over the other. But when she saw the rabbits' sad faces, she decided to help. She worked with the squirrels to fix the leak and save the rabbits' home.

As the storm passed, the hare realized that helping one family didn't mean she couldn't still play with the other. The rabbits appreciated her help, and the squirrels saw how kind she was. The hare learned that sometimes, when we take a chance and help others, we discover new paths and new friends.

# Fable 3

## Original (English)

In a land far, far away, beneath a shimmering waterfall, lived a magnificent eagle with wings as white as snow and eyes as bright as the stars. This generous eagle loved to help others and make the world a brighter place.

One day, the waterfall's creatures planned a grand festival to celebrate the changing of the seasons. They invited the eagle to join in, but just as the preparations were underway, someone sabotaged the festival by knocking over the colorful decorations and hiding the delicious food.

The eagle was devastated. Who could have done such a mean thing? She decided to investigate and soon discovered that a sly raccoon was behind the trouble. The raccoon wanted to be the center of attention and had ruined the festival to get everyone to look at her.

The eagle thought for a moment, "I could use my cleverness to outsmart the raccoon and get revenge." But then she remembered her friend, a wise old owl, who said, "Cleverness is like a sharp tool – it's only useful when guided by a kind conscience."

The eagle decided to take a different path. Instead of getting revenge, she worked with the other creatures to rebuild the festival and make it even stronger than before. When it was time to celebrate, the eagle dedicated a special memorial to those who had lost their belongings. "May their memories bring us together, not tear us apart," she said.

From that day on, the eagle's cleverness was guided by her kind heart, and the waterfall's creatures looked up to her as a true friend and leader.

# Fable 5

## Original (English)

High up on a mountain peak, where the air was crisp and the wind whispered secrets, a dramatic shrimp lived in a cozy little cave. This shrimp loved to perform for the creatures below, doing flips and tricks in the sunlight.

One day, a shy but clever rabbit climbed to the peak, searching for a legendary treasure. The shrimp saw the rabbit's worn-out shoes and kind eyes, and decided to share the cave and its treasures. Together, they explored the peak and discovered a hidden chest filled with shiny pebbles and sparkling crystals.

However, as time passed, the shrimp's love for the spotlight grew, and it started to perform less and less for the rabbit. The rabbit, feeling unappreciated, began to resent the shrimp. One day, the rabbit turned against its former friend and tried to take the treasure for itself.

The shrimp, heartbroken and confused, realized that its own selfishness had led to the rabbit's anger. With a newfound understanding, the shrimp approached the rabbit and asked, "Why are you so angry? We shared the cave and the treasure together. Why do you want to take it all for yourself?"

The rabbit's heart softened, and it explained that it felt unappreciated and sad. The shrimp listened and apologized, and together, they decided to share the treasure among all the creatures on the mountain peak. From that day on, the shrimp and the rabbit became the best of friends, and their friendship shone brighter than any treasure.

As the wind whispered its secrets again, the shrimp realized that empathy had softened the hardest of hearts, including its own.
